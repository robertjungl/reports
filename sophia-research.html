01 Executive Summary

💗 For Sophia

Bottom line: Mebendazole (Polymorph C) is the strongest candidate for your brain metastases. It's the only antiparasitic with confirmed blood-brain barrier penetration (B/P ratio 0.82), completed Phase I/II human safety trials, and direct evidence against breast cancer CNS metastases. It can be prescribed off-label by any physician and safely combined with your current abemaciclib regimen.

Patient Profile

  • Sophia Sanchez, 31 — ER+/PR+/HER2-, metastatic breast cancer
  • Brain: 10+ new mets (intraparenchymal, leptomeningeal, dural). Largest 1.3cm.
  • Bones: Diffuse mets throughout spine, sacrum, ilia, humerus, femur. SUV up to 14.1.
  • Systemic: New lymphadenopathy, pulmonary nodules, possible adrenal met, bilateral pleural effusions
  • LMD: Borderline. CTC CSF = 2 (threshold ≥3). Leptomeningeal enhancement on MRI.
  • Current Rx: Abemaciclib 100mg BID + Exemestane 25mg + Zoladex + Dexamethasone 2mg
  • Radiation: SRS started Feb 9, 2026 (Dr. Yao Yu)

🚨 Critical Finding: Missing Standard of Care

Sophia appears to be missing bone-protective therapy. With extensive bone mets (SUV 14.1), ASCO guidelines recommend Denosumab (Xgeva) 120mg SC every 4 weeks. This should be discussed with Dr. Robson urgently. Denosumab showed 18% reduction in skeletal events vs. zoledronic acid.

Key Conclusions from 10 Hours of Research

  1. Mebendazole Polymorph C is the top antiparasitic candidate — crosses BBB, Phase I/II data, safe at high doses, minimal CYP3A4 overlap with abemaciclib
  2. Fenbendazole has one remarkable ER+/HER2- case report (complete remission) but is a veterinary drug with no human trials and hepatotoxicity risk
  3. Ivermectin does NOT cross the BBB — unsuitable for brain mets but has immunomodulatory potential for systemic disease
  4. Disulfiram + copper is an overlooked BBB-penetrant option with cancer stem cell targeting
  5. Hydroxychloroquine may synergize with abemaciclib by blocking autophagy-mediated resistance
  6. HER2-low testing is urgent — if Sophia is HER2-low, trastuzumab deruxtecan (Enhertu) could be game-changing
  7. Abemaciclib is the most important drug in her regimen — only CDK4/6 inhibitor with confirmed CSF penetration
  8. No antiparasitic should REPLACE standard treatment — evidence supports ADJUNCTIVE use only

05 Mechanisms of Action & BBB Penetration

Mebendazole

  • Tubulin depolymerization — binds colchicine site, IC50 0.11-0.31 µM
  • Angiogenesis inhibition — inhibits VEGFR2
  • Hedgehog pathway inhibition — drives osteolytic bone mets
  • Anti-stemness — reduces ITGβ4, cancer stem cell markers
  • Radiation sensitization — inhibits DDR protein trafficking
  • NOT a P-gp substrate — reliable brain penetration
  • BBB: B/P 0.82 (Polymorph C) — brain conc. 29x IC50

Fenbendazole

  • Moderate tubulin destabilizer — less potent than colchicine
  • p53 activation — G2/M arrest
  • GLUT transporter downregulation — glucose starvation
  • Pyroptosis induction — HK2/caspase-3/GSDME pathway (2025)
  • NOT a P-gp substrate
  • BBB: Unknown — not studied in humans

Ivermectin

  • Immunogenic cell death — HMGB1, calreticulin exposure
  • Wnt/β-catenin inhibition — blocks EMT, metastasis
  • Cold→Hot tumor conversion — T-cell infiltration
  • PAK1 kinase inhibition
  • P-gp substrate — actively effluxed from brain
  • BBB: DOES NOT CROSS — 90x brain levels in P-gp KO mice

10 Other Repurposed Drugs

Disulfiram + Copper

BBB: ExcellentCYP: LOW risk

Chelates copper → proteasome inhibition, ALDH/CSC targeting, NF-κB inhibition. 250mg daily + Cu gluconate 2mg. GBM trial (400mg) was negative with toxicity — lower doses better tolerated. Must avoid ALL alcohol.

Metformin

BBB: ModerateCYP: BENEFICIAL

Abemaciclib increases metformin AUC by 37% (synergy). Crosses BBB. AMPK/mTOR inhibition, CSC targeting. MA.32 adjuvant trial was negative, but mechanistic synergy with CDK4/6i is real. Start 500mg daily, reduce for interaction.

Hydroxychloroquine

BBB: GoodCYP: LOW risk

Autophagy inhibitor — blocks CDK4/6 resistance mechanism. Phase I trial (2025) with palbociclib showed enhanced senescence at lower doses. NCT04523857 testing abemaciclib + HCQ specifically. 200-400mg BID. Monitor ophthalmology/QT.

Doxycycline

BBB: Low-ModCYP: LOW risk

Inhibits mitochondrial biogenesis in cancer stem cells. Reduces "stemness" ~63%. 100mg daily, alternate months with MBZ (COC protocol). Caution: antibiotic damages microbiome; one study suggested it could wake dormant breast cancer cells.

Niclosamide

BBB: Limited dataBioavail: POOR

Multi-pathway inhibitor (STAT3, Wnt, mTOR, NF-κB). Excellent mechanism but terrible oral bioavailability (t½ 11.8 min in human microsomes). Phase I found 500mg TID insufficient for therapeutic levels. NOT recommended for Sophia — better options exist.

Fluvastatin

BBB: ModerateCYP: CYP2C9 (safe)

ONLY statin option safe with abemaciclib (minimal CYP3A4). Atorvastatin/simvastatin are contraindicated. MASTER trial (Phase III) tested atorvastatin in ER+ BC. Fluvastatin 80mg in breast cancer window studies showed anti-tumor effects. 40mg daily if statin desired.
