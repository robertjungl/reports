🚫 Do NOT Take — Dangerous With Her Regimen

▶
Metformin CONTRAINDICATED
Why not: Abemaciclib increases metformin blood levels by 37% (AUC). This dramatically raises lactic acidosis risk — potentially fatal. Also, poor BBB penetration makes it useless for her brain mets. The CCTG MA.32 trial showed no benefit in non-diabetic breast cancer patients.
Itraconazole CONTRAINDICATED
Why not: Strong CYP3A4 inhibitor. Would cause dangerous accumulation of Verzenio (abemaciclib) in her blood, leading to severe toxicity — neutropenia, hepatotoxicity, fatal diarrhea. Never combine with CDK4/6 inhibitors.
Low-Dose Naltrexone (LDN) BLOCKED BY OXYCODONE
Immunomodulator / Opioid Growth Factor Receptor Agonist
LDN is a strong candidate — but only if oxycodone is discontinued.

What it does: At low doses (1-4.5mg), naltrexone briefly blocks opioid receptors, triggering a rebound increase in endorphins and enkephalins. This modulates the immune system, inhibits cancer cell proliferation via the opioid growth factor-receptor (OGFr) axis, and reduces IL-6 via TLR-7,8,9 pathways. 2024 review confirms anti-cancer properties.

BBB Penetration: Excellent — naltrexone freely crosses the blood-brain barrier.

Evidence: Growing body of case reports and small trials showing benefit in various cancers. Strong immunomodulatory rationale. Very safe, very cheap ($30-50/month compounded).

The problem: Naltrexone is an opioid antagonist. Sophia takes oxycodone for pain. LDN would block oxycodone's pain relief and could trigger acute opioid withdrawal (sweating, cramping, agitation, severe pain). These two drugs cannot coexist.

Path forward: If Sophia's pain management can transition away from opioids (e.g., to nerve blocks, gabapentin, or non-opioid protocols), LDN becomes viable and should be strongly considered. Discuss with her pain management team.
Sophia-specific: LDN is worth pursuing as a future option. If her SRS successfully treats brain mets and pain improves enough to taper off oxycodone, LDN could be added for immune support and anti-cancer effect. Crosses BBB, cheap, minimal side effects, no liver toxicity, no CYP3A4 interaction. The opioid conflict is the only barrier.
Cimetidine CONTRAINDICATED
Why not: Inhibits multiple CYP enzymes including CYP3A4. Would significantly increase Verzenio blood levels. Use famotidine or omeprazole instead if H2/PPI needed.
Berberine CAUTION — CYP3A4
Why not: Moderate CYP3A4 inhibitor that would increase Verzenio levels. Also has poor oral bioavailability and limited BBB penetration. The AMPK activation (similar to metformin) is not worth the interaction risk.
Ivermectin (Systemic) NOT RECOMMENDED
Why not: Does NOT cross the blood-brain barrier — useless for her brain mets, which are the primary threat. Also a CYP3A4 substrate that could compete with Verzenio metabolism. Case reports of IVM+MBZ combos exist, but IVM alone adds risk without CNS benefit.
Fenbendazole NOT RECOMMENDED
Why not: Highest hepatotoxicity risk of all benzimidazoles. Veterinary formulations with inconsistent dosing. The published breast cancer paper was retracted. Mebendazole Polymorph C is superior in every way — better BBB penetration, better safety profile, more clinical data.
Diclofenac NOT NOW
Why not: Well-known hepatotoxic NSAID. Risk of fulminant hepatitis. Absolutely contraindicated with her current elevated liver enzymes (AST 70, ALP 249). Low-dose aspirin provides similar anti-inflammatory benefit with far less liver risk.
Keto Diet MIXED EVIDENCE
Why not: October 2024 Columbia University study showed ketogenic diet increased lung metastases in breast cancer mouse models. Some RCTs show tumor size reduction, but the metastasis risk is unacceptable for Sophia who already has widespread metastatic disease. Do not aggressively pursue.
Vitamin D Supplementation STOP — TOXIC
Why not: Current level >150 ng/mL is toxic. Causing calcium elevation (10.1, creeping toward hypercalcemia). Can cause kidney damage and cardiac arrhythmias. STOP all Vitamin D supplements. Resume only after levels drop below 50 ng/mL (4-8 weeks).
