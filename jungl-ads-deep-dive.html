The Three Root Causes

1. Too Many Ad Sets, Not Enough Budget

Meta requires approximately 50 optimization events (purchases) per week per ad set to exit Learning Phase. With 14 ad sets sharing a $42/day budget, each ad set gets ~$3/day. At a $28-$60 cost per purchase, each ad set can generate maybe 0.05-0.1 purchases per day. That's 0.35-0.7 per week. You'd need 71-143x more budget per ad set to hit 50/week.

2. Constant Campaign Editing

Every time you change an ad set's budget, targeting, or creative, it restarts the Learning Phase. With 14 ad sets being turned on/off (I can see many were active then turned off), the algorithm never has a chance to learn and optimize.

3. Too Small Audience Pools for Remarketing

Your remarketing campaign reaches only 3,337 people. At a frequency of 5.62, the audience is saturated. The campaign says "Learning limited" — which means Meta CAN'T find enough people in the audience to optimize delivery.

The Fix (Simple)

Consolidate Everything

  • Minimum $50/day per ad set (ideally $100+)
  • Don't touch campaigns for 7 days after launching
  • Use Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns for prospecting — Meta handles all targeting
